The Abacus and Early Computing

Above is the ENIAC, or the Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer. This computer was composed of 18,000 vacuum tubes and weighed 30 tons! It took up a whole room and had many manual switches. It was built at the University of Pennsylvania by John Mauchly and J. Presper Ecker. It was built for the United States Miltary to perform calculations for WW2. It could add as many as 5,000 digits in a second. One main difference from a computer today is that it had no memory to store certain functions and commands.
